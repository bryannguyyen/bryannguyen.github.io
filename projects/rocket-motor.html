Solid Rocket Motor (TW-39)

Jan 2025 – Jun 2025 • Propulsion Lead

Overview: Led a 5-person propulsion subteam in the design, machining, testing, and integration of a custom solid rocket motor (TW-39). The project achieved a peak thrust of ~2000 N, total impulse of 5776 Ns, and successfully launched a dual-deployment rocket to over 7000 ft, placing fourth in the 2025 FAR-UNL competition.

Propellant Development

  • Formulated a sugar-based composite propellant with aluminum powder to improve combustion efficiency.
  • Performed small-scale static burns to measure burn rate and pressure characteristics.
  • Optimized mixture ratios for maximum specific impulse while maintaining safe chamber pressures.

Design & Manufacturing

  • Designed the motor casing, nozzle, and closure systems in Onshape with manufacturability and structural integrity in mind.
  • Machined steel and aluminum components using CNC and lathe processes.
  • Integrated o-ring sealing and pressure relief features for improved reliability and safety.

Cross-section CAD of the solid rocket motor assembly

Testing & Results

  • Conducted single-grain static fire tests to characterize burn performance and validate motor predictions.
  • Full-scale static test achieved ~2000 N peak thrust and a total impulse of 5776 Ns.
  • Validated MATLAB simulation models with empirical test data to refine performance predictions.

Challenges & Lessons Learned

  • Early nozzle erosion issues resolved by adjusting nozzle throat material and geometry.
  • Inconsistent grain casting resolved by developing a more controlled mixing and curing process.
  • Improved test data acquisition workflow for higher-fidelity thrust curve measurement.

Next Steps

  • Integrate new propellant formulation and nozzle redesign into next-generation motor for higher impulse class.
  • Automate data collection and analysis pipeline for future static fire tests.
